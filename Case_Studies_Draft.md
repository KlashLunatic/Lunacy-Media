# Lunacy Media Case Studies: Draft Content

## 1. Mulligan: The Signal System
**Category:** Brand Identity & Strategy
**The Vision:** To move a creative collective from a loose concept into a recognizable cultural force.
**The Challenge:** Mulligan was a group of high-level creators with no unified visual or narrative "signal." They needed a system that felt as sophisticated as their output but as raw as their origins.
**The Solution:** 
*   **Mythology:** We developed the "Signal" concept—the idea that the collective is a broadcast center for high-frequency creativity.
*   **Visual Identity:** A minimalist, high-contrast system using a custom "M" monogram that functions as a compass and a frequency wave.
*   **Outcome:** A complete brand manual, social media system, and investor deck that secured their first major studio partnership within three months.

## 2. Obeah: Contemporary Mythology
**Category:** Original IP / Graphic Novel
**The Vision:** A contemporary exploration of Caribbean mythology, spiritual power, and cultural identity.
**The Challenge:** Caribbean folklore is often relegated to "history" or "superstition." The goal was to recontextualize it as a functional, modern power system within a graphic novel framework.
**The Solution:** 
*   **Worldbuilding:** Created a 150-page world bible detailing the "Spiritual Architecture" of the Obeah universe, blending traditional wisdom with modern urban tension.
*   **Visual Language:** Developed a unique "Spiritual UI"—visual overlays that represent internal power and ancestral connection on the page.
*   **Outcome:** Volume 1 completed, successfully establishing a visual and narrative foundation for a multi-volume series and potential screen adaptation.

## 3. Tammy: The Mirror of Identity
**Category:** Original IP / Interactive Narrative
**The Vision:** A psychological journey through identity, memory, and transformation.
**The Challenge:** How to make the abstract concept of "self-construction" feel visceral and interactive in a digital space.
**The Solution:** 
*   **Interactive Narrative:** Built a prototype using a "Branching Identity" system, where user choices don't just change the plot, they change the visual representation of the protagonist.
*   **Atmosphere:** Utilized a "Digital Noir" aesthetic—high-contrast lighting, distorted audio, and symbolic iconography to represent psychological states.
*   **Outcome:** A playable narrative prototype that serves as a proof-of-concept for a full-scale immersive digital experience.
